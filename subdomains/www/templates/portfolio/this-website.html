Buying a Domain

I use namecheap. What can I say, I'm a victim of good branding. There are plenty of good options, but this tutorial is geared to namecheap.

How do I find a cheaper url?

You might notice coolwebsite.com costs almost $20,000 and feel like you have not shot at buying a cool domain. Despair not, there are still choices. You see if a domain is expensive that is because it is either already owned by someone or is considered "premium". You have two options.
1. pick a unique enough url that it won't be expensive
2. take advantage of less common TLD
TLD stands for Top Level Domain, its the ending of your url (.com, .net, .eu, .club, etc). There are a lot of TLDs and I won't get into full detail about them. Here are some popular ones. Also take advantage of this fun AI url generator.
Once you bought the url you need to setup the DNS, here is an example. Here you can see two types of records.
1. An "A Record" pointing to the IP address of the server
2. CNAME pointing to the "A Record"
My setup is a little fancy, but for the most simple you just need a CNAME record for www and an A Record for the server. You may not have an ip address of a backend, lets talk about that next.
